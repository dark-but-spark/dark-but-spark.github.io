Logic and Proofs

Logic

Logic is the basis of all mathematical reasoning

  • syntax of statements
  • meaning of statements
  • rules of logical inference

Propositional Logic

Proposition: a declarative statement that is either true or false(只有对和错)

  • declarative: making an explicit statement（陈述句）

Compound proposition（复合命题）

A compound proposition is a proposition that is built from multiple elementary propositions using logical connectives. 用多个简单命题通过连接词构成的命题。

elementary propositions using logical connectives: 连接词

Negation (not)

Let ppp be a proposition. The proposition “It is not the case that ppp.” is called the negation of ppp, denoted by ¬p\lnot p¬p, and read as “not ppp”.

Truth Table

ppp ¬p\lnot p¬p

F T

Conjunction (and)

Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The conjunction of ppp and qqq, denoted by p∧qp \land qp∧q, is true when both ppp and qqq are true and false otherwise.

Truth Table

ppp qqq p∧qp \land qp∧q
T T T
T F F
F T F
F F F

Disjunction (or)

Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The disjunction of ppp and qqq, denoted by p∨qp \lor qp∨q, is true when ppp or qqq is true and false otherwise.

Truth Table

ppp qqq p∨qp \lor qp∨q
T T T
T F T
F T T
F F F

Exclusive Or (xor)

Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The exclusive or of ppp and qqq, denoted by p⊕qp \oplus qp⊕q, is true when either ppp or qqq is true (i.e., exactly one of ppp, qqq is true) and false otherwise.

Truth Table

ppp qqq p⊕qp \oplus qp⊕q
T T F
T F T
F T T
F F F

Implication (if…then)

Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The implication (also known as conditional statement) “if ppp, then qqq”, denoted by p→qp \rightarrow qp→q, is false when ppp is true and qqq is false, and true otherwise.

  • ppp is called the hypothesis or premise and qqq is called the conclusion

Truth Table

ppp qqq p→qp \rightarrow qp→q ¬p∨q\lnot p \lor q¬p∨q
T T T T
T F F F
F T T T
F F T T
  • p→qp \rightarrow qp→q is logically equivalent to ¬p∨q\lnot p \lor q¬p∨q

equivalent ways

• if ppp then qqq
• ppp implies qqq
• ppp is sufficient for qqq
• qqq is necessary for ppp
• qqq follows from ppp
• qqq unless ¬p\lnot p¬p
• ppp only if qqq

converse, contrapositive and inverse

  • The converse of p→qp \rightarrow qp→q is q→pq \rightarrow pq→p
  • The contrapositive of p→qp \rightarrow qp→q is ¬q→¬p\lnot q \rightarrow \lnot p¬q→¬p
  • The inverse of p→qp \rightarrow qp→q is ¬p→¬q\lnot p \rightarrow \lnot q¬p→¬q
  • p→qp \rightarrow qp→q is logically equivalent to its contrapositive ¬q→¬p\lnot q \rightarrow \lnot p¬q→¬p

Biconditional (if and only if)

Let p and q be propositions. The biconditional statement (also known as biimplication) “p if and only if q”, denoted by p ↔ q, is
true if p and q have the same truth values, and false otherwise.
• the opposite of p ↔ q is exclusive or p ⊕ q
